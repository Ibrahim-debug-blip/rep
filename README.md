# Islamic Reformers of South Asia: Historical Profiles

A responsive, semantic HTML website dedicated to exploring the lives, thoughts, and socio-political movements of three prominent 18th and 19th-century Islamic reformers in the Indian subcontinent.

##  About the Project

This repository contains a multi-page static website designed to educate users on the history of Islamic revivalism in South Asia. The project specifically highlights the profound impact of three key historical figures:

*   **Shah Waliullah:** His efforts in translating the Quran into Persian and bridging sectarian divides.
*   **Syed Ahmad Barelvi:** His leadership of the Mujahideen Movement (Tehreek-e-Mujahideen) and jihad against oppressive rulers.
*   **Haji Shariatullah:** His foundation of the Faraizi Movement in Bengal, focusing on core Islamic duties and peasant rights.

The goal of this project is to present clean, accessible, and structured historical data using modern HTML best practices.

## 📚 Topics Covered per Reformer

### 1. Shah Waliullah (1703–1762)
*   Translation of the Holy Quran into Persian.
*   Letters to Ahmad Shah Abdali and the Battle of Panipat.
*   Theories on social justice and economic balance ("Al-Budur al-Bazighah").

### 2. Syed Ahmad Barelvi (1786–1831)
*   The creation of the Mujahideen military operational base.
*   The Battle of Balakot and its ultimate historical impact.
*   Purification of Sufi practices in the subcontinent.

### 3. Haji Shariatullah (1781–1840)
*   The declaration of Bengal as "Dar-ul-Harb" (Abode of War).
*   Emphasis on "Faraiz" (obligatory religious duties).
*   Socio-economic resistance against oppressive indigo planters and landlords.
